Chapter 1

🌱The Spark

I didnt stumble into tech—I studied it.

Three years. Diploma in Information Technology Management. Rosebank College. 2021 to 2023.

Somewhere between Introduction to Web Development and my first databse course, something clicked. Building things from scratch. Querying data. Watching structure emerge from chaos. It was the only part of my courseworks that didn't feel like work.

After graduating, life took a practical turn. I joined Robert Bosch as a learner in Production Technology. I operated machines. I learned what precision actually means. I showed up every day, even when it was repetitive, even when I was tired.

That learnership ended in March 2026. And while I was home, applying for jobs, the curiosity crept back in.

"What if I'm actaully got good at this?"

So here I am. Picking up where I left off. One line of code at a time.

Chapter 2

🏭What Manufacturing Taught Me About Code

At Bosch, I wasn't writing code. I was operating production machines.

But here's what I didn't expect: the lessons transferred.

🎯

Precision Matters

One wrong setting and the whole batch is compromised. Same with a missing semicolon.

🔁

Systems Thinking

Every machine is part of a bigger process. So is every function in an application.

🦾

Discipline Over Motivation

You show up. You do the work. even when it's boring. Even when you'd rather be anywhere else.

I'm bringing that same energy to learning CSS and JavaScript. Some days it's frustrating. I keep showing up anyway.

Chapter 9

🦾The Honest Truth

I won't pretend this is easy.

Some days CSS makes me want to close my laptop and watch another documentary. Other days JavaScript feels like a language I should already understand but I don't.

Motivation comes and goes. Time is always tight. Confusion is constant.

But I keep coming back.

Because I've seen what happens when I push through. Because I remember how it felt to build my first website in that college classroom—like I'd unlocked something.

Might as well try.
